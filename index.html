What is flexbox?

Flexbox is a layout mode that determines the position and the size of elements based on the way they interact with their parent (the container) and their siblings (the other elements).

How do we use flexbox?

To start, give the container element a display of flex. This will put all of the containerâ€™s child elements within one row.

Properties can then be applied to either the parent (the container) or the children (the elements directly under the container)

Parent Properties

Here are some properties that can be applied to the parent component.

  • flex-direction

    values: row | row-reverse | column | column-reverse

  • flex-wrap

    values: nowrap | wrap | wrap-reverse

  • justify-content

    values: flex-start | flex-end | center | space-between | space-around

  • align-items

    flex-start | flex-end | center | baseline | stretch

Child Properties

Here are some properties that can be applied to the child elements.

  • order

    value: A number representing the order where the child should be. The default is 1.

  • flex

    value: A number representing the size of the element in relation to the other siblings within the container

  • align-self

    values: auto | flex-start | flex-end | center | stretch
